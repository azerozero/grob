## Context

What situation are we in? What problem exists? What triggered this work?

## Goals

What must this solution achieve? Be specific and measurable.

## Non-Goals

What is explicitly OUT OF SCOPE? This is the most valuable section -- it prevents scope creep and clarifies thinking.

## Proposed Design

The solution. Describe it at the right level of abstraction -- enough to evaluate tradeoffs, not so much it is pseudo-code.

## Alternatives Considered

For each alternative:
- What is it?
- Why is it a reasonable option?
- Why did we NOT choose it?

The rejected alternatives are as important as the chosen one.

## Risks and Mitigations

What could go wrong? How do we detect it? How do we recover?

## Open Questions

What do we not know yet? What needs more investigation? Honest uncertainty is better than false confidence.
